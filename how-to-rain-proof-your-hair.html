How to rain-proof your hair

Sarah Jessica Parker in 'Sex and the City' 2002, Getty Images

Frizz, flatness, tangles: rain not only plays havoc with your mood and sartorial choices, it also has consequences for your hair. We spoke to the experts to find out how to avoid and combat the common problems a downpour can cause for your locks, so that you'll never have to suffer a rain-induced bad hair day again.

1. Add volume

"Volume starts and ends at the roots", says Tom Connell, artistic director at Trevor Sorbie. "If the elements have flattened your hair, resetting the roots is the main way to achieve a fuller look. Take a hairdryer (with nozzle attached) and, beginning at the parting, dry the root in the opposite direction to which the hairs naturally fall." Connell also explains that your hair's finish will differ depending on the brush you choose; "a paddle brush minimises volume, whereas a round brush will encourage it". As a result, opt for the latter before "turning your head upside down and applying Redken's Wind Blown Dry Finishing Spray, £12.99, throughout".

Using dry shampoo can also "give that instant injection of life to any look," says Michael Lendon, Aveda Advanced's creative director. "If your hair is suddenly looking a little lacklustre in the volume department, apply Aveda's Shampure Dry Shampoo, £21.89, underneath your roots to create lift", or follow Moroccanoil UK education manager Vanessa Boland's example and keep "Moroccanoil's Dry Shampoo, £13, in your handbag to help revive and rescue lifeless soggy hair if caught in the rain."

2. Prep properly

"I always tell my clients that to get the best results it's like prepping a canvas", says celebrity hair stylist Paul Edmonds. "You need to start with the right shampoo and conditioner, such as L'Oréal Professionnel's Series Expert Liss Unlimited Shampoo, £13.81, and Masque, £13.90. Then, if the weather is bad, pre-empt flattening by applying Kérastase's L'Incroyable Blow Dry Lotion, £21.12," as, when heat-activated, this lotion allows you to restyle your hair without applying additional product.

3. Lock in moisture

One of the most common rain-induced woes is frizz but its habitual presence in our lives doesn't have to be guaranteed. For the best results, start by "prepping your hair with Moroccanoil's Moroccanoil Treatment, £14.30, to help lock in moisture," says Boland, or follow our how to tame frizzy hair guide- an easy eight-step guide to help transform your tresses.

If you have curly hair, or hair that is damaged from heat styling or colour, you may find that your hair is more prone to frizz, explains Collier. "This hair often has lifted cuticles in the structure, which allows water to penetrate the strands easily." He recommends, "using a moisturising masque that is "enriched with oils and silicones" like Kerastase's Nutritive Masque Magistral, £22.25, while Lendon suggests Aveda's Smooth Infusion Smoothing Masque, £22.73, to nourish dry tresses.

4. Comb, don't brush

Getting caught in the rain is not only annoying but it can leave you with hair that is particularly tangled as well as wet. As award-winning celebrity hairstylist Jason Collier highlights, "When your hair is wet, it's more prone to damage and breakage, and while on the go, it's easy to mistreat it in a hurry, by pulling at it with a brush." Instead, you should "start by towel drying your hair gently (no rubbing!), before combing it through with a wide-tooth comb, like Ghd's Detangling Comb, £7.50, starting from your ends and slowly working your way up to the roots."

If you hair is particularly prone to matting, Katie Allan, Creative Manager at Charles Worthington Salons, also suggests "applying L'Oréal Professionnel's Mythic Oil, £11.75, into your hair as a pre-shampoo treatment," to help keep knots to a minimum.

5. Blow-dry as soon as you're out of the shower

"If your hair reacts badly to winter weather", says Connell "begin drying your hair as quickly as possible after shampooing. The temporary bonds in the hair start to form as soon as you have turned off the shower so the quicker you start to form them into the shape you'd like the better chance you'll have of achieving the desired style."

To make your blow-dry last longer, you can also try a pre-shampoo treatment like Triple Impact³, £30, suggests Corrine D'Souza, Head of Education at Pera|357. "Vulnerable hair absorbs moisture causing perfectly blow dried hair to lose its shape in damp weather", she explains. "Triple Impact³, on the other hand, reduces the absorption of humidity in poor weather, giving your blow-dried hair staying power."

6. Use a hair masque overnight

"To get extra help in fighting the frizz, you should start the night before," says Edmonds. If you know you are washing your hair when you wake up, try applying Kérastase's Discipline Maskeratine, £25.60, or Shu Uemura's Shusu Sleek Masque, £47.94, before twisting your hair into a bun to sleep and rinsing it out in the morning."

For days when you don't want to wash your hair, "a good tip at night is to take a small amount of leave-in conditioner, such as Aveda'sDamage Remedy Daily Hair Repair, £22.89, and apply evenly through damp hair, focusing on the ends", as Lendon recommends. Again, twist your hair into a loose bun as you go to bed, so that when you release your locks in the morning "you'll be left with hair that has frizz-free body and shine."

7. Experiment with updos

"On a wet morning, there is no better and more tried and tested look than the ponytail", says Connell. "But there are ponytails and then there are ponytails." To ensure you achieve the latter, he recommends using a tail comb, such as Kent's Fine Hair Tail Comb, £7.52, to gently backcomb a rectangular section of your hair just below the crown. Then, smooth hair from the crown over the top and secure it "with an elastic tight in the nape of your neck", before spritzing on Ouai's Texturising Hair Spray, £34.98. Or, try smoothing Allan's approach and use "a clean blusher brush dipped in some L'Oréal Professionnel's Tecni.Art Anti-Frizz Spray, £11.04, to control any flyaways.

When it comes to casually beautiful hair, though, it seems our French cousins provide the strongest inspiration. If you have long hair, Collier recommends "an undone Parisian kind of look" in the form of "a messy up-do inspired by your favourite French film star. It's slightly dishevelled nature will ensure that you remain looking chic whatever the weather." To try out a similar style, head to our how-to video for a braided up-do. Or if your hair is shorter, Edmonds suggests "a tousled French undone look" created by "applying a dab of Shu Uemura's Kengo Feather, £22, to the hair at the mid lengths and ends... because if worn with the right attitude no one will question it."
