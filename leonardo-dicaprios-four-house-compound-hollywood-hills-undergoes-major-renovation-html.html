Leonardo DiCaprio's four-house compound in the Hollywood Hills undergoes major renovation

Leonardo DiCaprio's lavish four property compound in the Hollywood Hills is getting a drastic makeover - with the actor getting the opportunity to acquire a fifth home for his extensive estate.

The Oscar-winner, 48, who is worth an estimated $300million, has been slowly building up his estate since purchasing his main home for $2.5million more than two decades ago -  a 4,500-square-foot hilltop house in the Bird Streets neighborhood, which boasts jaw-dropping views and an infinity pool.

His main home looks to be undergoing a major renovation, with a digger seen in the patio, a skip full of building material in the driveway and an empty pool.

The interior also appears to be undergoing a renovation. A building permit recently connected to it states the home is being reroofed to comply with regulations as it is in a Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zone.

DiCaprio first moved into the Oriole Way compound - which is actually two side-by-side properties - in 1994. One of the homes was Madonna's former digs.

In December 2022, the star bought a $10.5million 3,500 square-foot, four bedroom, four bathroom home in the celebrity enclave, Bird Streets of Hollywood Hills - which belonged to his former neighbors, Ron and Joan Linclau - who he famously battled with over a basketball court.

The 1963 home was never listed publicly for sale, with the property located on the northern line of his compound. It boasts a pool and a guesthouse.

DiCaprio used his cousin Robert Hrtica's limited liability company to purchase the home.

In 2007 the Linclaus filed a lawsuit claiming DiCaprio's basketball court which he built on his property 'destabilized  and caused significant structural damage to their property.'

DiCaprio argued the issues were sparked by an illegal retaining wall the Linclaus had installed.

The lawsuit would later be settled out of court with the Linclaus and the actor continuing to be next door neighbors. 

Last year Joan died aged 90 with her widower Ron selling the home to DiCaprio.
